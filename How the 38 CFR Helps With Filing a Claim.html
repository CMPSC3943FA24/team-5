What to List on VA Form 21-526EZ

When filling out VA Form 21-526EZ, consider including the following information:

  • Your Medical Conditions: Clearly state all medical conditions for which you are seeking compensation. This includes any disabilities related to your service. Common conditions include:
    • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
    • Hearing Loss
    • Tinnitus
    • Knee Injuries (e.g., Limitation of Flexion)
    • Back Injuries (e.g., Lumbosacral or Cervical Strain)
  • Supporting Evidence: Gather and submit all relevant medical records, including:
    • Doctor's reports and evaluations
    • Service records
    • Any previous diagnoses related to the claimed conditions
  • Secondary Conditions: If you have secondary conditions that are related to your primary conditions, list those as well. For example, if you have tinnitus, you might also mention associated conditions like anxiety or depression.
  • Impact on Daily Life: Describe how your medical conditions affect your daily life and ability to work. This can strengthen your case.
  • Dependent Information: If you have dependents, include their information as it may impact your compensation.

Tips for Completing VA Form 21-526EZ

Here are some helpful tips to consider while filling out the form:

  • Be Thorough: Provide as much detail as possible for each condition to avoid any ambiguity.
  • Use Clear Language: Avoid technical jargon; use simple language that is easy to understand.
  • Review Before Submitting: Double-check your application for completeness and accuracy.
  • Seek Assistance: Consider reaching out to a VA-accredited representative or veteran service organization for help in completing the form.
