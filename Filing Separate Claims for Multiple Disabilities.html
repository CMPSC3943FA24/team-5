Filing Separate Claims for Multiple Disabilities

If you have multiple health conditions, it’s important to file separate claims for each one to ensure each disability is properly evaluated and compensated. Filing claims individually can improve the accuracy of your compensation.

Steps for Filing Separate Claims

Step 1: Gather Medical Evidence

  • Collect medical records, doctor’s reports, and any documentation that shows the link between your condition and your service.

Step 2: Use VA Form 21-526EZ

  • File VA Form 21-526EZ for each condition separately. Include supporting evidence for each claim to strengthen your case.

Step 3: List Each Condition Separately

  • Ensure that each condition is clearly listed on the form and supported with specific evidence for better chances of approval.

Step 4: Submit Your Claims

  • File each claim online via the VA portal, by mail, or in person at a VA office.

Key Considerations When Filing Multiple Claims

Medical Documentation

  • Make sure each condition has its own set of supporting medical documentation.

List Conditions Separately

  • Don’t group conditions together; clearly outline each one individually for better evaluation.

Use Specific Language

  • Explain how each condition impacts your daily life and your ability to work.

Check 38CFR Criteria

  • Ensure you are aware of the 38CFR regulations that relate to your condition and back your claims with the right criteria.

Benefits of Filing Separate Claims

More Accurate Ratings

  • Each condition gets evaluated based on its own merit, leading to potentially more accurate disability ratings.

Better Understanding

  • Filing separately helps the VA better understand the full extent of your health issues.

Increased Compensation

  • Combining ratings for multiple conditions may result in higher overall compensation.
