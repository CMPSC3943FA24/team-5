Can You File a Claim if You Were Never Deployed?

Yes, even if you were never deployed, you can still file a claim for a disability rating. If you received a diagnosis while on active duty, you are entitled to seek VA disability compensation for that condition.

Requirements for Filing a Claim

  • Diagnosis Received During Active Duty: You must have been diagnosed with a condition while serving on active duty.
  • Service Connection: The diagnosis must be linked to your military service, even if it did not occur in a combat or deployment situation.
  • Medical Records: Provide medical documentation from your service records that support your diagnosis and its relation to your time in service.

How to File Your Claim

To file a claim for a diagnosis received during active duty, follow these steps:

  • Gather all medical records related to your diagnosis and treatment while on active duty.
  • Complete the VA Form 21-526EZ â€“ Application for Disability Compensation.
  • Submit your claim through the eBenefits portal or mail your application to the VA.

What to Expect After Filing

Once your claim is submitted, the VA will review your medical records and service connection. If approved, you will receive a disability rating that reflects the severity of your condition and its impact on your daily life.
