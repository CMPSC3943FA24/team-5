To be eligible for the BDD (Benefits Delivery at Discharge) program, you must reach all of these requirements.

  • You must be a full-time service member in active duty (the National Guard, Reserve, and Coast Guard count as well).
  • You know your separation date.
  • Your separation date is between 90-180 days away.

Along with your claim application, you will need to provide a copy of your service treatment record for your service as well as a Separation Health Assessment form. The disability claims application and the separation health assessment form are located below.

Click here to view the disability claims application Click here to view the Separation Health Assesment form

You can file your claim online, in person or in the mail. After you submit your application, you must be available for the next 45 days after the date you submit your claim to go in for a VA medical exam. You also must finish all phases of the VA and Defense Department medical separation examination process before you leave the military.

Things that may exclude you from the BDD program are:

  • You need case management for a serious injury or illness
  • You are terminally ill
  • You are waiting to be discharged while being treated at a VA hospital or military treatment facility
  • You are waiting for your character of discharge to be determined
  • You’re not able to go to the VA in the 45 days after you file your claim
  • You didn’t submit copies of your service treatment records from your time in service
  • You added a medical condition to your original claim when you had less than 90 days left in service (they will process the added conditions after your discharge)
  • You need to have a VA exam done in a foreign country and the VA can’t request the exam through the BDD offices in Landstuhl, Germany, or Camp Humphreys, Korea

If you need help applying, you can contact your local Transition Assistance Office as well as call the VA at (800)-827-1000.

If you have less than 90 days of service left, you can file a fully developed claim or a standard disability claim.
If you are wounded, injured, or ill and can't perform your duties, your doctor will refer you to the IDES (Integrated Disability Evaluation System.
